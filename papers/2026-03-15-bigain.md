## Abstract
Acceleration methods for diffusion models (e.g., token merging or downsampling) typically optimize synthesis quality under reduced compute, yet often ignore discriminative capacity.

We revisit token compression with a **joint objective** and present **BiGain**, a **training-free, plug-and-play framework** that preserves generation quality while improving classification in accelerated diffusion models.

## Key Insight: Frequency Separation
Our key insight is **frequency separation**: mapping feature-space signals into a frequency-aware representation disentangles:
- **Fine detail** (high frequency) → important for generation
- **Global semantics** (low frequency) → important for classification

This enables compression that respects both generative and discriminative requirements.

## Benefits
- **Training-free**: No additional training required
- **Plug-and-play**: Works with existing diffusion models
- **Dual objective**: Maintains both generation quality and classification accuracy
- **Efficient**: Reduces computation while preserving capabilities

## Results
Experiments show BiGain:
- Preserves generation quality under token compression
- Improves classification accuracy compared to naive compression
- Achieves better trade-offs than existing methods
