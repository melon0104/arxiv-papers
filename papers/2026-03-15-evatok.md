## Abstract
Autoregressive (AR) video generative models rely on video tokenizers that compress pixels into discrete token sequences. The length of these token sequences is crucial for balancing reconstruction quality against downstream generation computational cost.

Traditional video tokenizers apply a **uniform token assignment** across temporal blocks of different videos, often:
- **Wasting tokens** on simple, static, or repetitive segments
- **Underserving** dynamic or complex ones

## EVATok Framework
To address this inefficiency, we introduce **EVATok** (**E**fficient **V**ideo **A**daptive **Tok**enization):

### Key Innovation: Adaptive Token Allocation
- **Content-aware**: Allocates more tokens to complex/dynamic segments
- **Adaptive length**: Variable token count based on content complexity
- **Efficient**: Reduces overall token count while maintaining quality

### Benefits
- Better reconstruction quality per token
- Lower computational cost for simple segments
- Improved efficiency for video generation pipelines

## Results
- Significant reduction in average token count
- Maintained or improved reconstruction quality
- Faster video generation with AR models
