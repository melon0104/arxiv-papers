## Abstract
Unified multimodal models target joint understanding, reasoning, and generation, but current image editing benchmarks are largely confined to natural images and shallow commonsense reasoning, offering limited assessment of this capability under **structured, domain-specific constraints**.

## GRADE Benchmark
We introduce **GRADE**, the first benchmark to assess **discipline-informed knowledge and reasoning** in image editing.

### Dataset
- **520 carefully curated samples**
- **10 academic domains**: spanning natural science to social science
- Requires domain-specific knowledge to edit correctly

### Evaluation Framework
Multi-dimensional evaluation assessing:
- **Domain knowledge understanding**
- **Reasoning about physical/scientific constraints**
- **Edit quality and accuracy**

### Examples
- Physics: Editing shadows to match light sources
- Biology: Correcting anatomical structures
- Chemistry: Adjusting molecular visualizations
- Geography: Modifying maps correctly

## Why Notable
- **First domain-knowledge editing benchmark**: Goes beyond natural image editing
- **Multi-domain coverage**: 10 academic disciplines
- **Full resources released**: Code, dataset, and leaderboard available
