# TriRec: A Tri-Party Framework for Agent-Based Recommendation

- **arXiv**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10673
- **PDF**: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.10673
- **Categories**: cs.IR
- **Published**: 2026-03-11

## Authors
Yaxin Gong, Chongming Gao, Chenxiao Fan, Wenjie Wang, Fuli Feng

## Abstract
Recent advances in large language models (LLMs) have stimulated growing interest in agent-based recommender systems, enabling language-driven interaction and reasoning for more expressive preference modeling. However, most existing agentic approaches remain predominantly **user-centric**, treating items as passive entities and neglecting the interests of other critical stakeholders. This limitation exacerbates exposure concentration and long-tail under-representation, threatening long-term system sustainability.

In this work, we identify this fundamental limitation and propose the first **Tri-party LLM-agent Recommendation framework (TriRec)** that explicitly coordinates:
- **User utility**
- **Item exposure**
- **Platform-level fairness**

## Architecture
The framework employs a **two-stage architecture**:

### Stage 1: Item Self-Promotion
Empowers item agents with personalized self-promotion to improve matching quality and alleviate cold-start barriers.

### Stage 2: Platform-Level Re-ranking
Uses a platform agent for sequential multi-objective re-ranking, balancing user relevance, item utility, and exposure fairness.

## Key Findings
Experiments on multiple benchmarks show consistent gains in accuracy, fairness, and item-level utility.

**Important insight**: Item self-promotion can simultaneously enhance fairness and effectiveness, challenging the conventional trade-off assumption between relevance and fairness.

## Why Notable
- **First tri-party recommendation framework**: Balances user, item, and platform interests
- **LLM-powered agents**: Novel use of agentic systems in recommendations
- **Challenges trade-off assumptions**: Fairness and accuracy can improve together
