## Abstract
The ability of generative AI (GenAI) methods to photorealistically alter camera images has raised awareness about the authenticity of images shared online. Interestingly, images captured directly by modern cameras using on-device GenAI features (e.g., object removal, sky replacement) pose a novel authenticity challenge: they are simultaneously 'straight out of camera' yet genetically modified. We provide a systematic analysis of this problem from Meta's perspective, examining how camera-integrated GenAI blurs the line between authentic and synthetic photography, and propose principles for image authentication frameworks that accommodate this new reality.
