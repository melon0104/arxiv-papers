# Low-Rank Adaptation Redux for Large Models

## 概要（日本語）
LoRAはパラメータ効率的なファインチューニング（PEFT）の事実上の標準となっているが、理論的な理解や最適化の観点では改善の余地がある。本研究は「LoRA Redux」と称してLoRAの再定式化・改善を行い、より大きなモデルへのスケーラビリティと理論的保証を強化した新しいLoRAバリアントを提案。

- 実用的なFine-tuning手法の改善

## Abstract
Low-rank adaptation (LoRA) has emerged as the de facto standard for parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) of foundation models, enabling the adaptation of billion-parameter networks with minimal computational overhead. Despite its widespread adoption, LoRA's theoretical foundations and optimization behavior remain incompletely understood, particularly for very large models. We revisit LoRA from first principles and propose LoRA Redux, a reformulated approach that provides stronger theoretical guarantees, better optimization landscapes, and improved scalability to models with hundreds of billions of parameters.
